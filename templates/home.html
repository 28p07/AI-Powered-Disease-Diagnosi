Disease Information

Diabetes

Diabetes affects how your body processes glucose. Left untreated, it can cause serious health problems.

  • Increased thirst and urination
  • Extreme hunger and weight loss
  • Blurred vision
Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to various conditions that affect the heart, including arrhythmias and coronary artery disease.

  • Chest pain and palpitations
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness and sweating
Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a common type of cancer that can develop in both men and women, primarily affecting women.

  • New lump or thickening in the breast
  • Redness or skin irritation
  • Pain or pulling of the nipple
Chronic Kidney Disease

Chronic kidney disease can damage your kidneys slowly, leading to waste buildup in your body.

  • Fatigue and nausea
  • Muscle cramps
  • Loss of appetite
Liver Disease

Liver disease encompasses a range of liver conditions, including cirrhosis and hepatitis.

  • Abdominal pain
  • Unexplained bruising
  • Fatigue and nausea
Malaria

Malaria is caused by parasites transmitted through mosquito bites and can lead to severe health complications.

  • Fever and chills
  • Headache and fatigue
  • Nausea and vomiting
Pneumonia

Pneumonia is an infection that causes inflammation of the lungs. It can cause severe respiratory issues if left untreated.

  • Cough with mucus
  • High fever and fatigue
  • Chest pain and sore throat
